3.6 Other terms and definitions

For the purposes of this document, the following terms and definitions apply:

3.6.1
product structure

characterization of the composition of a product which can be a hierarchical representation where all assembly relationships are explicit or a partly implicit one where some nodes are not related to a product but to a family of products with relationships conditioned by boolean expressions

EXAMPLE 1   In the first approach, a part A would be specified as resulting from the assembly of a part B and a part C.

EXAMPLE 2   The description of the product structure of products with a huge diversity, like vehicles proposed by major car manufacturers, is usually done using the second approach.
