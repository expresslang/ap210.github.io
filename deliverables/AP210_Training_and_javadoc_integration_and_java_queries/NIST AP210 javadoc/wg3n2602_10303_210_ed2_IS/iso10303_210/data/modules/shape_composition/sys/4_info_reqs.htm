4 Information requirements

This clause specifies the information requirements for the Shape composition application module. The information requirements are specified as the Application Reference Model (ARM) of this application module.

NOTE 1  A graphical representation of the information requirements is given in Annex C.

NOTE 2  The mapping specification is specified in 5.1. It shows how the information requirements are met by using common resources and constructs defined or imported in the MIM schema of this application module.

The following EXPRESS specification begins the Shape_composition_arm schema and identifies the necessary external references.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
SCHEMA Shape_composition_arm;
(*

4.1 Required AM ARM

The following EXPRESS interface statement specifies the elements imported from the ARM of another application module.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
USE FROM Shape_property_assignment_arm;    --  ISO/TS 10303-1032
(*

NOTE 1   The schemas referenced above are specified in the following part of ISO 10303:

Shape_property_assignment_arm ISO/TS 10303-1032

NOTE 2   See Annex C, Figures C.1and C.2 for a graphical representation of this schema.

4.2 ARM entity definitions

This subclause specifies the ARM entities for this module. Each ARM application entity is an atomic element that embodies a unique application concept and contains attributes specifying the data elements of the entity. The ARM entities and definitions are specified below.

4.2.1 Composite_group_shape_element   

A Composite_group_shape_element is a type of Composite_shape_element in which the composite may be thought of as a group of individual Shape_element in which any property that is applied to the Composite_group_shape_element is applied to each of the constituent Shape_elements on an individual basis.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Composite_group_shape_element
  SUBTYPE OF (Composite_shape_element);
END_ENTITY;
(*

4.2.2 Composite_shape_element   

A Composite_shape_element is a type of Shape_element that is composed of two or more constituent Shape_elements. A Composite_shape_element is either a Composite_group_shape_element or a Composite_unit_shape_element.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Composite_shape_element
  ABSTRACT SUPERTYPE OF (ONEOF (Composite_group_shape_element,
                                Composite_unit_shape_element))
  SUBTYPE OF (Shape_element);
INVERSE
  composing_relationships : SET[2:?] OF Shape_element_composing_relationship FOR relating;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

composing_relationships: an inverse relationship that specifies that the existence of the Composite_shape_element is dependent on the existence of the Shape_element_composing_relationship that specifies the Composite_shape_element as its relating. There shall be two or more Shape_element_composing_relationship for a Composite_shape_element.

4.2.3 Composite_unit_shape_element   

A Composite_unit_shape_element is a type of Composite_shape_element that is treated as a unit, as opposed to as a group of individual Shape_element.

EXAMPLE 1   A Surface_profile_tolerance is applied to a square cut-out that goes through a printed circuit board. The square cut-out is a Composite_unit_shape_element that consists of the four constituent Shape_elements that correspond to the four surfaces of the cut-out.

EXAMPLE 2   A Flatness_tolerance may be applied to a Composite_unit_shape_element to specify that the coplanar surfaces that make up the Composite_unit_shape_element are required to fall within a common Tolerance_zone.

NOTE    Common tolerance zones are described in 7.5 of ISO 1101.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Composite_unit_shape_element
  SUBTYPE OF (Composite_shape_element);
END_ENTITY;
(*

4.2.4 Shape_element_boundary_relationship   

A Shape_element_boundary_relationship is a type of Shape_element_relationship that specifies that a Shape_element is the boundary or a portion of the boundary of another Shape_element. The related specifies the Shape_element that is the boundary or a portion of a boundary of the Shape_element specified as the relating.

NOTE 1   The attribute related is declared in the Shape_element_relationship entity of which this entity is a subtype.

NOTE 2   The attribute relating. is declared in the Shape_element_relationship entity of which this entity is a subtype.

EXAMPLE    A Shape_element_boundary_relationship may be used to specify that a Shape_element is one of the boundaries of a rectangular Datum_target area. The boundary may then be located relative to a Datum with a Location_dimension.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Shape_element_boundary_relationship
  SUBTYPE OF (Shape_element_relationship);
WHERE
  WR1: NOT EXISTS (SELF\Shape_element_relationship.relation_type);
END_ENTITY;
(*

Formal propositions:

WR1: The relation_type shall not be populated.

4.2.5 Shape_element_composing_relationship   

A Shape_element_composing_relationship is a type of Shape_element_constituent_relationship that associates a Composite_shape_element with a Shape_element from which the Composite_shape_element is composed.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Shape_element_composing_relationship
  SUBTYPE OF (Shape_element_constituent_relationship);
  SELF\Shape_element_relationship.relating : Composite_shape_element;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

relating: an instance specifies the Composite_shape_element that is the Shape_element that is composed from the Shape_element specified as the related for the Shape_element_composing_relationship.

4.2.6 Shape_element_constituent_relationship   

A Shape_element_constituent_relationship is a type of Shape_element_relationship that associates a Shape_element with a second Shape_element to which the first Shape_element is a constituent. The Shape_element that is the constituent Shape_element is specified as the related. The Shape_element to which the constituent Shape_element is a constituent is specified as the relating. A Shape_element_constituent_relationship may be a Shape_element_composing_relationship.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Shape_element_constituent_relationship
  SUPERTYPE OF (Shape_element_composing_relationship)
  SUBTYPE OF (Shape_element_relationship);
UNIQUE
  UR1: SELF\Shape_element_relationship.related, SELF\Shape_element_relationship.relating;
WHERE
  WR1: SELF\Shape_element_relationship.related <> SELF\Shape_element_relationship.relating;
  WR2: NOT EXISTS (SELF\Shape_element_relationship.relation_type);
END_ENTITY;
(*

Formal propositions:

UR1: The combination of the relating inherited from Shape_element_relationship and related inherited from Shape_element_relationship shall be unique within a population of Shape_element_constituent_relationship. That is, any specific Shape_element shall not be a constituent of any one Shape_element more than once.

WR1: The Shape_element that is specified as the relating shall not be a constituent of itself, either directly or indirectly.

WR2: The relation_type shall not be populated.

4.2.7 Shape_element_locating_relationship   

A Shape_element_locating_relationship is a type of Shape_element_constituent_relationship that is the association between two Shape_elements in which the associated Shape_elements are located in relation to each other.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Shape_element_locating_relationship
  SUBTYPE OF (Shape_element_constituent_relationship);
  SELF\Shape_element_relationship.relating RENAMED locating_shape_element : Shape_element;
  SELF\Shape_element_relationship.related RENAMED located_shape_element : Shape_element;
UNIQUE
  UR1: located_shape_element, locating_shape_element;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

locating_shape_element: specifies the Shape_element in relation to which the Shape_element specified by the located_shape_element is located for the Shape_element_locating_relationship.

located_shape_element: specifies the Shape_element that is located in relation to the Shape_element specified by the locating_shape_element for the Shape_element_locating_relationship.

Formal propositions:

UR1: The combination of located and locating Shape_elements shall be unique within a population of Shape_element_locating_relationship. That is, the same Shape_elements shall not be located relative to each other more than once.



*)
END_SCHEMA;  -- Shape_composition_arm
