3.6 Other terms and definitions

For the purposes of this document, the following terms and definitions apply:

3.6.1
B-stage

an intermediate stage in the reaction of a thermosetting resin in which the material melts when heated and dissolves in certain solvents

3.6.2
C-stage

the final stage of the curing of a thermosetting resin in which the material has become infusible and insoluble in common solvents

3.6.3
conformal coat

a protective, dielectric coating designed to conform to the surface of a PWA or PCA

3.6.4
glue mask

a rigid plate with a pattern of voids to create glue patterns during a manufacturing process

3.6.5
laminate

a product made by bonding together two or more layers of material or materials

3.6.6
paste mask

a rigid plate with a pattern of voids used to create solder paste patterns during a manufacturing process. Solder paste is deposited only where there are voids in the plate

3.6.7
preimpregnated

A combination of mat, fabric, nonwoven material, or roving with resin, usually advanced to the B-stage, ready for curing

3.6.8
semi-cured

B-stage

3.6.9
semi-fluid

material flow properties intermediate between liquids and solids

3.6.10
silkscreen

a material layer realized by transferring an image to a surface by forcing suitable media through a stencil screen with a squeegee

3.6.11
solder mask

an insulating layer of a PCB or PWB that exposes only the areas to be soldered

3.6.12
solder paste

a specially blended paste consisting of carefully graded solder powder particles, and a flux medium designed to give the paste particular printing and reflow characteristics

3.6.13
stratum

one layer of material in the sequence of materials that compose a PCB, and that usually has an aspect ratio that is very large, typically 1,000:1
