4 Information requirements

This clause specifies the information requirements for the Part and zone laminate tables application module. The information requirements are specified as the Application Reference Model (ARM) of this application module.

NOTE 1  A graphical representation of the information requirements is given in Annex C.

NOTE 2  The mapping specification is specified in 5.1. It shows how the information requirements are met by using common resources and constructs defined or imported in the MIM schema of this application module.

The following EXPRESS specification begins the Part_and_zone_laminate_tables_arm schema and identifies the necessary external references.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
SCHEMA Part_and_zone_laminate_tables_arm;
(*

*)

4.2 ARM type definitions

This subclause specifies the ARM types for this application module. The ARM types and definitions are specified below.

4.2.1 component_class_for_assembly   

The component_class_for_assembly type allows for the designation of the data types Composite_assembly, Filament_laminate, Ply, Ply_laminate and Processed_core.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
TYPE component_class_for_assembly = SELECT
   (Composite_assembly,
    Filament_laminate,
    Ply,
    Ply_laminate,
    Processed_core);
END_TYPE;
(*

4.2.2 thickness_laminate_table_component   

The thickness_laminate_table_component type allows for the designation of the data types Filament_laminate, Ply and Processed_core.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
TYPE thickness_laminate_table_component = SELECT
   (Filament_laminate,
    Ply,
    Processed_core);
END_TYPE;
(*

4.3 ARM entity definitions

This subclause specifies the ARM entities for this module. Each ARM application entity is an atomic element that embodies a unique application concept and contains attributes specifying the data elements of the entity. The ARM entities and definitions are specified below.

4.3.1 Composite_assembly   

A Composite_assembly is a type of Constituent_part. hat is made of multiple materials that are bonded together. A Composite_assembly may be made from any combination of materials. A completed Composite_assembly need not be a rigid shape. A Composite_assembly may also be used as a composite part.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Composite_assembly
  SUBTYPE OF (Constituent_part);
  layup_part : Composite_assembly_table;
  shape : OPTIONAL composite_assembly_shape;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

layup_part: specifies a role of the Composite_assembly_table for the Composite_assembly.

shape: specifies a role of the composite_assembly_shape for the Composite_assembly. The value of this attribute need not be specified.

4.3.2 Composite_assembly_sequence_definition   

A Composite_assembly_sequence_definition is a single unique sequence or layer within a Composite_assembly. The sequence or layer contains one or more constituent parts. When more than one constituent part resides in the same sequence or layer, the constituents must not overlap.

NOTE    If constituents do overlap they must be put on separate sequence or layers.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Composite_assembly_sequence_definition;
  components_in_sequence : BAG[1:?] OF component_class_for_assembly;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

components_in_sequence: specifies a role of the component_class_for_assembly for the Composite_assembly_sequence_definition. There shall exist one or more component_class_for_assembly for the Composite_assembly_sequence_definition

4.3.3 Composite_assembly_table   

A Composite_assembly_table is a type of Part_laminate_table that provides an ordered list of constituent parts. The ordered list provides basic placement position within the laminate (see Figure 1 ).




Figure 1 —  Composite assembly table

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Composite_assembly_table
  SUBTYPE OF (Part_laminate_table);
  sequence_groups : LIST[2:?] OF Composite_assembly_sequence_definition;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

sequence_groups: specifies a role of the Composite_assembly_sequence_definition for the Composite_assembly_table. There shall exist not less than two Composite_assembly_sequence_definitions for the Composite_assembly_table.

4.3.4 Constituent_part   

A Constituent_part is a type of Part_view_definition. A Constituent_part may, but need not be, defined in a component-assembly relationship. A Constituent_part may be either a Ply or a Ply_piece, or a Ply_laminate, or a Filament_laminate, or a Processed_core, or a Composite_assembly.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Constituent_part
  SUPERTYPE OF (ONEOF (Ply,
                       Ply_piece,
                       Ply_laminate,
                       Filament_laminate,
                       Processed_core,
                       Composite_assembly))
  SUBTYPE OF (Part_view_definition);
  SELF\Product_view_definition.id RENAMED constituent_part_identification : STRING;
  weight : OPTIONAL Mass_data_element;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

constituent_part_identification: an attribute inherited from the Product_view_definition specifies the composite item.

weight: quantifies the mass of the Constituent_part. The value of this attribute need not be specified.

4.3.5 Edge_zone_shape   

An Edge_zone_shape is a type of Zone_structural_makeup_shape_representation that depicts the outline of a zone or area of a part.

NOTE    An Edge_zone_shape might not be a distinguishable physical edge. It could be where a smooth transition in thickness of the part starts or stops.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Edge_zone_shape
  SUBTYPE OF (Zone_structural_makeup_shape_representation);
  boundary : boundary_class_zone;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

boundary: specifies a role of the boundary_class_zone for the Edge_zone_shape.

4.3.6 Filament_laminate   

A Filament_laminate is a type of Constituent_part that is a bonded material of two or more bundles of filament construction. The Filament_laminate defines a volume to fill with filament assemblies.




Figure 2 —  Filament laminate

NOTE 1   The shape of a Filament_laminate may be defined by a cross section and a length.

NOTE 2   When a Filament_laminate is made from a Filament_assembly, it usually refers to individual tows, yarns, and rovings. These volumes can be as simple as the cross section and length of a radius filler to as complex as a filament wound vessel. Filament_laminates that are made by pulling and forming processes, called pultrusions, may use a tape form of Filament_assembly.

NOTE 3   The difference between a ply and a Filament_laminate, when referencing a Filament_assembly, is that a ply describes a constant thickness area to be filled by a Filament_assembly and a Filament_laminate describes a general volume to be filled by a Filament_assembly.

EXAMPLE    Pultruded shapes, radius fillers, and filament wound structure are types of Filament_laminate (see Figure 2 ).

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Filament_laminate
  SUBTYPE OF (Constituent_part);
  made_from : Filament_assembly;
  shape : OPTIONAL Filament_laminate_shape;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

made_from: specifies the stock Filament_assembly that the Filament_laminate is made from. The Filament_assembly is in the form of a tow, yarn or roving, and sometimes tape.

shape: specifies a role of the Filament_laminate_shape for the Filament_laminate. The shape specifies the geometry that defines the cross-sectional area of a Filament_laminate. The value of this attribute need not be specified.

4.3.7 Filament_laminate_shape   

A Filament_laminate_shape is a a two-dimensional geometric shape representation for the cross-sectional area of a Filament_laminate. It is a two-dimensional geometric shape representation for the cross-sectional area of a Filament_laminate.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Filament_laminate_shape;
  cross_section : Shape_description_association;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

cross_section: represents the cross-sectional area of a Filament_laminate.

4.3.8 Laminate_table   

A Laminate_table is a type of Part_view_definition. A Laminate_table defines the internal constituents or structural makeup of an overall part or a particular zone, area, or point on the part. A Laminate_table may either a Part_laminate_table or a Zone_structural_makeup.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Laminate_table
  SUPERTYPE OF (ONEOF (Part_laminate_table,
                       Zone_structural_makeup))
  SUBTYPE OF (Part_view_definition);
  basis : Reinforcement_orientation_basis;
  resulting_surface : OPTIONAL Surface;
  base_surface : OPTIONAL Surface_with_direction;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

basis: specifies specifies the orientation direction for the load carrying reinforcements within the Laminate_table.

resulting_surface: specifies the opposing Surface that forms from the physical material being placed on the base_surface. The value of this attribute need not be specified.

base_surface: specifies a role of the Surface_with_direction used to define the lamination or layers. The layers or lamination are defined to start from the surface and to be laminated or layered in the specified direction and order. The value of this attribute need not be specified.

4.3.9 Part_laminate_table   

A Part_laminate_table is a type of Laminate_table that applies to the overall laminate. It is used to provide allocation of each physical constituent through the use of an ordered placement list. A Part_laminate_table may be either a Composite_assembly_table or a Ply_laminate_table.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Part_laminate_table
  SUPERTYPE OF (ONEOF (Composite_assembly_table,
                       Ply_laminate_table))
  SUBTYPE OF (Laminate_table);
END_ENTITY;
(*

4.3.10 Percentage_laminate_table   

A Percentage_laminate_table is a type of Zone_structural_makeup. that defines the percentage of the total thickness within a zone or point that each uniquely oriented constituent stock material makes up.

NOTE    For a simple laminate just made out of plies (Ply_laminate), a Percentage_laminate_table is used to capture the percentages of different fiber orientations within the zone based on the overall thickness of the zone.

EXAMPLE    An example would be 40% 0 degree fibers, 30% 90 degree fibers, 15% +45 degree fibers, and 15% -45 degree fibers. The sum of the percents (%) have to equal 100.

The sum of volume_percent must equal 100.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Percentage_laminate_table
  SUBTYPE OF (Zone_structural_makeup);
  table_components : SET[1:?] OF Percentage_ply;
  total_thickness : Length_data_element;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

table_components: specifies a role of the Percentage_ply for the Percentage_laminate_table. There shall exist one or more Percentage_ply for the Percentage_laminate_table.

total_thickness: specifies a role of the Length_data_element for the Percentage_laminate_table.

4.3.11 Percentage_ply   

A Percentage_ply is an idealization of a ply that represents a portion (percentage) of the thickness of a Percentage_laminate_table with a particular fiber orientation (if required) and material type. This represents a way to relate anisotropic material properties to the varied orientations that exist in a composite part. A Percentage_ply always corresponds to the area within the zone of the Laminate_table of which it is a portion.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Percentage_ply;
  strength_orientation : OPTIONAL Ply_orientation_angle;
  material : Stock_material;
  makeup_and_properties : OPTIONAL Zone_structural_makeup;
  volume_percent : Ratio_data_element;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

strength_orientation: specifies a role of the Ply_orientation_angle for the Percentage_ply. The value of this attribute need not be specified.

material: specifies a role of the Stock_material for the Percentage_ply.

makeup_and_properties: specifies a role of the Zone_structural_makeup for the Percentage_ply. The value of this attribute need not be specified.

volume_percent: specifies a role of the Ratio_data_element for the Percentage_ply.

4.3.12 Ply   

A Ply is a type of Constituent_part that defines one of the layers of constant thickness material that make up a composite part. A Ply is a contiguous structure with a defined boundary that is made of one or more Ply_piece objects (see Figure 3 ).




Figure 3 —  Ply

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Ply
  SUBTYPE OF (Constituent_part);
  constituents : OPTIONAL LIST[1:?] OF Ply_piece;
  ply_thickness : Length_data_element;
  material_orientation : Direction_of_fiber_orientation_within_a_ply;
  material_type : ply_stock_material;
  shape : OPTIONAL Ply_shape;
DERIVE
  material_orientation_angle : Ply_orientation_angle := material_orientation.orientations[1];
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

constituents: specifies the Ply_piece objects that make up the Ply. There shall exist one or more Ply_pieces for the Ply. The value of this attribute need not be specified.

ply_thickness: specifies the thickness that the Ply will contribute to part thickness after curing. The ply_thickness is constant over the whole area of the Ply.

material_orientation: specifies the direction of the reinforcement fibers within a Ply. For materials with multiple orientations of fibers, orientation shall correspond to the warp direction of the material.

material_type: specifies the ply_stock_material that the Ply is made of.

shape: specifies the Ply_shape for the Ply. The value of this attribute need not be specified.

material_orientation_angle: specifies the Ply_orientation_angle for the Ply.

4.3.13 Ply_laminate   

A Ply_laminate is a type of Constituent_part that is formed by two or more Ply objects that mate with each other. The plies have unique orientation and shape within the Ply_laminate.




Figure 4 —  Ply laminate

NOTE    Examples of Ply_laminate shape include angle, cap, flat, general contour, simple contour, complex contour, channel, etc. (see Figure 4 ).

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Ply_laminate
  SUBTYPE OF (Constituent_part);
  shape : OPTIONAL ply_laminate_shape;
  ply_table : Ply_laminate_table;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

shape: specifies a role of the ply_laminate_shape for the Ply_laminate. The value of this attribute need not be specified.

ply_table: specifies a role of the Ply_laminate_table for the Ply_laminate.

4.3.14 Ply_laminate_sequence_definition   

A Ply_laminate_sequence_definition is an illustration of a single unique sequence or layer within a Ply_laminate.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Ply_laminate_sequence_definition;
  plies_in_sequence : SET[1:?] OF Ply;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

plies_in_sequence: specifies the Ply objects that are laminated at the sequence. There shall exist one or more Ply for the Ply_laminate_sequence_definition.

4.3.15 Ply_laminate_table   

A Ply_laminate_table is a type of Part_laminate_table that provides an ordered list of Ply objects. The ordered list provides basic placement position within the laminate (see Figure 5 ).

NOTE 1   The valid components of a Ply_laminate_table are plies only.

NOTE 2   The Ply_laminate_table is only valid to describe a Part_laminate_table.




Figure 5 —  Ply laminate table

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Ply_laminate_table
  SUBTYPE OF (Part_laminate_table);
  sequence : LIST[2:?] OF Ply_laminate_sequence_definition;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

sequence: specifies a role of the Ply_laminate_sequence_definition for the Ply_laminate_table. There shall not less than two Ply_laminate_sequence_definitions for the Ply_laminate_table.

4.3.16 Ply_piece   

A Ply_piece is a type of Constituent_part. It is a single portion of a Ply that may be combined with other ply pieces on the same layer to make up the Ply. A Ply_piece has the same material and orientation direction as defined for the Ply of which it is a portion.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Ply_piece
  SUBTYPE OF (Constituent_part);
  shape : OPTIONAL ply_piece_shape;
  warp_surface : LOGICAL;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

shape: specifies a role of the ply_piece_shape for the Ply_piece. The value of this attribute need not be specified.

warp_surface: specifies whether or not the material warp surface is away from the basis surface, when the value of this attribute is set to true or false respectively. Material warp surface is the side of the material where the majority of the fibers are located.

4.3.17 Point_zone_shape   

A Point_zone_shape is a type of Zone_structural_makeup_shape_representation that specifies a point on the surface of a part. It also specifies the singular volume a piercing line would make as it traveled through the part, normal to the part's surface at the point.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Point_zone_shape
  SUBTYPE OF (Zone_structural_makeup_shape_representation);
  location : point_select;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

location: specifies the point_select that defines the position of the local pierced point of the Point_zone_shape.

4.3.18 Processed_core   

A Processed_core is a type of Constituent_part that is the central component of a sandwich construction to which faces or skins are attached. The primary structural purpose of Processed_core is the transfer of shear loads between the faces or skins. Processed_core is Stock_core that has been machined, formed, stabilized and/or bonded together (see Figure 6 ). A core may have a potting compound or adhesive applied to it to make it more rigid and/or provide solid attachment points.




Figure 6 —  Processed core

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Processed_core
  SUBTYPE OF (Constituent_part);
  added_material : OPTIONAL LIST[0:?] OF Ply;
  cell_direction : Direction_of_fiber_orientation_within_a_ply;
  made_from_stock : Stock_core;
  made_from_processed_core : OPTIONAL SET[1:?] OF Processed_core;
  shape : OPTIONAL core_shape;
DERIVE
  cell_orientation_angle : Ply_orientation_angle := cell_direction.orientations[1];
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

added_material: specifies a role of the Ply for the Processed_core. The Ply identifies the area where the added_material is applied. There sall exist zero or more Ply for the Processed_core. The value of this attribute need not be specified.

cell_direction: identifies the ribbon direction of the honeycomb core.

made_from_stock: specifies the Stock_core core that is processed to get the Processed_core configuration. The types of processes include machining, forming, stabilizing, and bonding.

made_from_processed_core: specifies a role of the Processed_core that has another process performed on it. There shall exist one or more Processed_cores for the Processed_core. The value of this attribute need not be specified.

shape: specifies a role of the core_shape for the Processed_core. The value of this attribute need not be specified.

cell_orientation_angle: specifies the Ply_orientation_angle for the Processed_core.

4.3.19 Smeared_material   

A Smeared_material is a type of Zone_structural_makeup that provides the material properties for an idealized zone or area of a part.

NOTE    A Smeared_material can represent the material properties of a single Stock_material by having the Percentage_laminate_table reference one Stock_material and set its percent value of that material’s constituent to 100%.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Smeared_material
  SUBTYPE OF (Zone_structural_makeup);
  total_thickness : Length_data_element;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

total_thickness: specifies the thickness or depth of the zone defined by the Smeared_material.

4.3.20 Surface_with_direction   

A Surface_with_direction is the specification of a Surface and a Direction. The Direction defines the side of the Surface that is the material side.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Surface_with_direction;
  defining_surface : Surface;
  material_direction : Direction;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

defining_surface: specifies the Surface that is used in the definition.

material_direction: specifies a role of the Direction for the Surface_with_direction.

4.3.21 Thickness_laminate_table   

A Thickness_laminate_table is a type of Zone_structural_makeup that is used to capture the ordered list of constituents that make up a zone.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Thickness_laminate_table
  SUBTYPE OF (Zone_structural_makeup);
  table_components : LIST[2:?] OF thickness_laminate_table_component;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

table_components: specifies the thickness_laminate_table_component that make up the zone. There shall exist not less than two thickness_laminate_table_components for the Thickness_laminate_table.

4.3.22 Zone_structural_makeup   

A Zone_structural_makeup is a type of Laminate_table that describes the structural makeup of a particular zone, area, or point within a laminate. A Zone_structural_makeup may be either a Thickness_laminate_table and a Smeared_material, or a Percentage_laminate_table and a Smeared_material, or a Thickness_laminate_table or a Percentage_laminate_table or a Smeared_material.

NOTE 1   Structural makeup information consists of: 1) the smear material properties based on one or more constituents that reside in that zone or point; 2) the percentage of the total thickness within a zone or point that each constituent type makes up; and 3) the ordered list of physical constituents based on position within a zone or point. These three pieces of structural makeup information can coexist in any order to describe a zone or point within a laminate.

NOTE 2   A single Zone_structural_makeup could apply to the whole part. The would be the case for a homogeneous structural part or a simple laminate of constant thickness and material constituents.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Zone_structural_makeup
  SUPERTYPE OF (ONEOF ((Thickness_laminate_table
               AND Smeared_material), (
                  Percentage_laminate_table
              AND Smeared_material),
                 Thickness_laminate_table,
                 Percentage_laminate_table,
                 Smeared_material))
  SUBTYPE OF (Laminate_table);
  boundary : OPTIONAL Zone_structural_makeup_shape_representation;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

boundary: specifies a role of the Zone_structural_makeup_shape_representation for the Zone_structural_makeup. The boundary specifies the shape of the Zone_structural_makeup as either a point, one or more unique list of curves, or a face. When the boundary is not defined for an instance of a Zone_structural_makeup, the Zone_structural_makeup applies to the whole part. Then would be the case for a homogeneous structural part.

4.3.23 Zone_structural_makeup_shape_representation   

A Zone_structural_makeup_shape_representation is the shape of a zone or area of a part.

NOTE    The zone can be a point, a specific area, or the entire part.

A Zone_structural_makeup_shape_representation may be either a Point_zone_shape or a Edge_zone_shape.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Zone_structural_makeup_shape_representation
  SUPERTYPE OF (ONEOF (Point_zone_shape,
                       Edge_zone_shape));
END_ENTITY;
