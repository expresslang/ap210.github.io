4 Information requirements

This clause specifies the information requirements for the Inertia characteristics application module. The information requirements are specified as the Application Reference Model (ARM) of this application module.

NOTE 1  A graphical representation of the information requirements is given in Annex C.

NOTE 2  The mapping specification is specified in 5.1. It shows how the information requirements are met by using common resources and constructs defined or imported in the MIM schema of this application module.

The following EXPRESS specification begins the Inertia_characteristics_arm schema and identifies the necessary external references.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
SCHEMA Inertia_characteristics_arm;
(*

4.2 ARM entity definitions

This subclause specifies the ARM entities for this module. Each ARM application entity is an atomic element that embodies a unique application concept and contains attributes specifying the data elements of the entity. The ARM entities and definitions are specified below.

4.2.1 Centre_of_mass   

A Centre_of_mass is a type of Shape_dependent_property_representation that represents the point characterizing a center of mass in a geometric coordinate space.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Centre_of_mass
  SUBTYPE OF (Shape_dependent_property_representation);
WHERE
  WR1: SIZEOF(Shape_dependent_property_representation.property_representation.items)=1;
  WR2: SIZEOF(QUERY (it <* Shape_dependent_property_representation.property_representation.items |'INERTIA_CHARACTERISTICS_ARM' + '.CARTESIAN_POINT' IN TYPEOF(it)))=1;
  WR3: 'INERTIA_CHARACTERISTICS_ARM' + '.GEOMETRIC_COORDINATE_SPACE' IN TYPEOF(Shape_dependent_property_representation.property_representation.context_of_items);
END_ENTITY;
(*

Formal propositions:

WR1: The associated representation shall contain only one item.

WR2: The only item of the associated representation shall be a Cartesian_point, locating the considered centre of mass.

WR3: The context_of_items of the associated representation shall be a Geometric_coordinate_space. The centre point is founded in that Geometric_coordinate_space.

4.2.2 Inertia_matrix_item   

An Inertia_matrix_item is a type of Representation_item that specifies the six distinct values of an inertia matrix which represents the angular mass of a rigid body about a particular point and their common unit.

These six values include three mass moments of inertia about three mass products of inertia about three orthogonal axes through the point in question.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Inertia_matrix_item
  SUBTYPE OF (Representation_item);
  inertia_values : ARRAY[1:6] OF REAL;
  common_unit : Unit;
END_ENTITY;
(*

Attribute definitions:

inertia_values: the six distinct values of the inertia matrix, in the following order: Ixx, Iyy, Izz, Ixy, Iyz, Izx.

x, y and z relate respectively to the first, second and third axis of the Axis_placement, according to which the inertia values have been computed.




Figure 1 —  Matrix of inertia representation

Figure 1 shows the spatial representation of the matrix of inertia of a rigid body.

common_unit: the Unit in which the inertia values are expressed.

4.2.3 Moments_of_inertia   

A Moments_of_inertia is a type of Shape_dependent_property_representation which measures the solid's ability to resist changes in rotational speed about a specific axis.

The moment of inertia of a body is not only related to its mass but also the distribution of the mass throughout the body. So two bodies of the same mass may possess different moments of inertia.

The matrix of inertia is specified with respect to a particular Axis_placement that identifies the axes along which the inertia values have been computed.

Ixx, Iyy and Izz are called the moments of inertia while Ixy, Iyx, Iyz, Izy, Izx and Ixz are the products of inertia.




Figure 2 —  Example of rigid body.




Figure 3 —  Mathematical formulas of the moments and products of inertia.

EXPRESS specification:

*)
ENTITY Moments_of_inertia
  SUBTYPE OF (Shape_dependent_property_representation);
WHERE
  WR1: SIZEOF(QUERY (it <* Shape_dependent_property_representation.property_representation.items |'INERTIA_CHARACTERISTICS_ARM' + '.AXIS_PLACEMENT'IN TYPEOF(it)))=1;
  WR2: SIZEOF(QUERY (it <* Shape_dependent_property_representation.property_representation.items |'INERTIA_CHARACTERISTICS_ARM' + '.INERTIA_MATRIX_ITEM' IN TYPEOF(it)))=1;
  WR3: 'INERTIA_CHARACTERISTICS_ARM' + '.GEOMETRIC_COORDINATE_SPACE' IN TYPEOF(Shape_dependent_property_representation.property_representation.context_of_items);
END_ENTITY;
(*

Formal propositions:

WR1: The associated representation shall contain only one Axis_placement.

WR2: The associated representation shall contain one Inertia_matrix_item.

WR3: The context_of_items of the associated representation shall be a Geometric_coordinate_space. The Axis_placement is founded in that Geometric_coordinate_space.



*)
END_SCHEMA;  -- Inertia_characteristics_arm
